## PART 2: P2P DISTRIBUTED GOVERNANCE ORGANIZATION (DGO)

The DGO is a P2P governance system with no central authority. Decisions are made at the lowest level of impact and propagate up through scalar propagation.

### 2.1 The Six-Level Strat

```
Level 1: Community in Town (neighborhood)
  ├─ Decisions: Local waste processing, local product distribution, local conflicts
  ├─ Voters: Only people in that town
  ├─ Authority: Decisions affect only that town
  └─ Scalar Propagation: Decisions propagate up if they affect larger communities

Level 2: Community in Borough (city district)
  ├─ Decisions: Borough-wide coordination, cross-town resource sharing
  ├─ Voters: Only people in that borough
  ├─ Authority: Decisions affect only that borough
  └─ Scalar Propagation: Decisions propagate up if they affect larger communities

Level 3: Community in City (city)
  ├─ Decisions: City-wide coordination, inter-borough resource sharing
  ├─ Voters: Only people in that city
  ├─ Authority: Decisions affect only that city
  └─ Scalar Propagation: Decisions propagate up if they affect larger communities

Level 4: Community in Defined Region (region)
  ├─ Decisions: Regional coordination, inter-city resource sharing
  ├─ Voters: Only people in that region
  ├─ Authority: Decisions affect only that region
  └─ Scalar Propagation: Decisions propagate up if they affect larger communities

Level 5: Community Cross-Regional (continental)
  ├─ Decisions: Cross-regional coordination, resource balancing
  ├─ Voters: Only people affected by cross-regional issues
  ├─ Authority: Decisions affect multiple regions
  └─ Scalar Propagation: Decisions propagate up if they affect global systems

Level 6: Community Across Defined Land (global)
  ├─ Decisions: Only truly global issues (GSWT minting formula, Gaia stewardship allocation)
  ├─ Voters: Only people affected by global decisions
  ├─ Authority: Decisions affect all humanity
  └─ Scalar Propagation: Highest level; affects all lower levels
```

### 2.2 Governance Principles

**Principle 1: Subsidiarity** Decisions are made at the lowest level of impact. A town makes decisions that affect only the town. A region makes decisions that affect only the region. Only truly global issues are decided globally.

**Principle 2: Consensus-Seeking** All decisions aim for 90%+ agreement (consensus), not 50%+ (majority rule). Minority concerns are documented and protected.

**Principle 3: Scalar Propagation** Decisions propagate up the hierarchy only if they affect larger communities. A town decision that affects only the town does not propagate. A town decision that affects the borough propagates to the borough level.

**Principle 4: Transparency** All decisions are recorded on GSWB (immutable). All votes are public. All reasoning is documented. No backroom dealings.

**Principle 5: Accountability** All decision-makers are accountable to those affected by their decisions. Recall and referendum mechanisms prevent capture.

### 2.3 Decision-Making Process

**Step 1: Proposal Submission** Any person can propose a decision. The proposal must include:

- Clear description of what is being proposed

- Why it is being proposed (the problem it solves)

- Expected outcomes and impacts

- Resource requirements (if any)

- Timeline for implementation

- Which communities are affected

**Step 2: Stakeholder Input** All affected parties have 1 week to provide input. Input is documented and attached to the proposal.

**Step 3: Deliberation** The relevant council discusses the proposal. Goal: consensus-seeking (90%+ agreement). Proposal is modified to address concerns.

**Step 4: Vote** If consensus is achieved (90%+ agreement), proposal is approved and implemented immediately. If not, proposal is either modified and re-discussed, or rejected.

**Step 5: Scalar Propagation** If the decision affects larger communities, it propagates to the next level. The process repeats at each level.

**Step 6: Implementation** Once approved at all affected levels, the decision is implemented. GE9 monitors outcomes.

**Step 7: Review** Quarterly review of decision outcomes. Adjustments made if needed.

### 2.4 Safeguards Against Capture

**Safeguard 1: Recall** Any elected official can be recalled with 50%+ popular vote. No official is safe from accountability.

**Safeguard 2: Referendum Override** Any decision can be overridden with 66%+ popular vote. Ultimate power rests with the people.

**Safeguard 3: Term Limits** All officials have term limits and cannot be re-elected to the same position. Prevents entrenchment.

**Safeguard 4: Sortition** Council members are selected via random lottery, not elections. Prevents wealth from influencing selection.

**Safeguard 5: Voting Power Formula** Voting power = f(staked_GSWT, reputation_score, contribution_history). Wealth does not translate directly to power.
